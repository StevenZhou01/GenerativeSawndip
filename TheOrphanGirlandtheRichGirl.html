The Orphan Girl and the Rich Girl ("the Zhuang Cinderella")

This is a story written in the generative characters

Use the mouse to see the characters' meaning
















Zhuang people have used Sawndip characters to write various literature. Among the Sawndip literature, “the Orphan Girl and the Rich Girl” is a fairy tale that is regarded as very similar to the Western story Cinderella. The Zhuang title is "Dahgyax Dahbengz". “Dah” indicates female, “gyax” means orphan, and “bengz” means rich. This paragraph is adapted from the story of “the Orphan Girl and the Rich Girl” and written by the generative characters. From it, we can see the connections between different cultures.


The Zhuang texts:

Dahgyax caeuq Dahbengz boh doengz meh mbouj doengz. Dazgya bohmeh dai, Dahbengz meh hangz Dazgya. Hawfwen, Dahbengz meh hawj Dazgya ndaw ranz. Dazgya meh hoiz roegga bang Dazgya daep buh gyaeu caeuq haiz gim. Dazgya hwnj Hawfwen, bae haiz. Sai aeu haiz ra Dazgya. Gajlaeng gyoengqde moq.


The English translation:

Dagya and Dabeng were half-sisters. After Dagya's parents passed away, her stepmother abused her. On the day of the Hawfwen Festival, the stepmother let Dagya stay at home. Her biological mother turned into a crow to help Dagya and asked Dagya to wear gold earrings, green silk clothes, and golden shoes to go to the festival. To avoid the handsome prince, she lost one shoe. The prince used the shoe to find Dagya. The two fell in love and got married.
